Our Organization

Purpose

Our Mission

The Ross Preschool is a play-based, nature-oriented preschool serving children ages two through five. Our curriculum is intentionally designed to build social-emotional development, creativity, character, and a love of learning in the most formative years of a child's life.

We operate as a nonprofit because we believe high-quality early education should be shaped by what children need – not by what pays. Every decision we make is guided by what's best for our children and families, not a profit margin.

How We Operate

Governance Policies

The Ross Preschool maintains formal written policies governing how we operate, make decisions, and handle information. These policies are reviewed regularly by the Board of Trustees.

Conflict of Interest Policy

All trustees and key personnel annually disclose potential conflicts of interest. Decisions involving potential conflicts are recused and documented in board minutes. Policy available upon request.

Whistleblower Protection Policy

Employees and trustees are encouraged to report concerns about financial, legal, or ethical matters. Reports are handled confidentially and without retaliation. Policy available upon request.

Document Retention & Destruction Policy

TRP maintains a written policy governing retention, secure storage, and destruction of financial, operational, and personnel records in compliance with California and federal requirements.

Code of Ethics

TRP operates under a clear set of ethical expectations for trustees, staff, and contractors – covering honesty, fiduciary duty, and the welfare of the children in our care.

To request copies of any governance policy document, contact david@therosspreschool.org.

Stewardship

How We Use Funds

TRP's resources come from three primary sources: tuition, charitable contributions, and investment income. Each plays a specific role in how we serve children.

Tuition

The majority of our revenue comes from tuition paid by families of enrolled children. Tuition directly supports classroom instruction, teacher compensation, facilities, and the everyday operation of the school.

Charitable Contributions

Donations from families, alumni, and the broader community support our two highest-priority investments: tuition assistance for families who cannot afford full tuition, and teacher compensation and retention – the single greatest driver of quality in early childhood education.

Investment Income

A modest portion of our revenue comes from responsibly managed reserves, which provide operational stability and long-term financial health.

Tuition Assistance

Marin County is one of the highest-cost regions in the country. As a nonprofit, we exist to serve our community. Approximately 15% of our families currently receive tuition assistance. Our goal is to expand access further as philanthropic support grows.

If tuition is a barrier, please reach out. We welcome the conversation.
