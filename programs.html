Who We Serve

Preschool & Pre-Kindergarten, Ages 2–5

The Ross Preschool offers a Preschool program for children ages 2–4 and a Pre-Kindergarten program for children ages 4–5. Both programs share our core philosophy - play-based, child-directed learning in a warm, nurturing environment - while the Pre-K program includes a deeper focus on kindergarten readiness skills.

Our progressive curriculum is designed to address the full developmental spectrum: cognitive, social-emotional, physical, and creative. With a special focus on 21st Century Skills, every experience at TRP is designed to help your child grow in ways that will matter for the rest of their life.

We are proud to be a 501(c)3 non-profit, which means every decision we make is guided by what's best for children and families - not a profit margin.

How Learning Really Happens

Play with Purpose

At TRP, play and learning are never separate. Every experience is intentional - designed to develop real skills in ways children never recognize as "work" because they're too busy having fun.

When children run a lemonade stand, they're practicing math, communication, negotiation, and the joy of generosity - all in one afternoon. When they tend the garden, they're learning science, patience, and the satisfaction of growing something with their own hands. When they build in the block area, they're doing physics and engineering. When they work through a conflict with a friend, they're developing empathy and problem-solving that will matter for the rest of their lives.

This is what "play-based" really means at TRP - not unstructured free time, but richly layered experiences where children are the authors of their own learning, guided by teachers who know exactly what's happening developmentally in every moment.

Signature Feature

Our Outdoor Classroom

Our outdoor classroom is one of TRP's most defining features. Children spend meaningful time outside every day - exploring, creating, and connecting with the natural world in ways that simply can't happen inside four walls.

The garden is a living classroom: kids tend herbs like mint, lavender, sage, oregano, and thyme, and watch over fruit trees including cherry, apple, lemon, and fig - harvesting what they've grown and bringing it straight to the mud kitchen. It sparks wonder, builds environmental stewardship, and makes every day outside feel like a genuine adventure.

What Children Experience

A Rich, Multi-Dimensional Curriculum

Our curriculum weaves together multiple domains of learning - ensuring each child develops holistically across intellectual, social, physical, and creative dimensions.

🎨

Process-Based Art

Children explore materials freely, developing fine motor skills, creative expression, and confidence. Art at TRP is rarely about the product - it's about the process and the joy of creating.

📚

Literacy & Language

Through stories, songs, rich conversation, and imaginative play, children develop a love of language - the foundation of everything that comes next in learning.

🔬

Science & Discovery

Sensory bins, hands-on experiments, and open-ended exploration fuel children's natural curiosity and teach the habits of mind: questioning, observing, and discovering.

🧮

Math & Logic

Through patterns, building, counting, and collaborative problem-solving, children develop mathematical thinking in ways that feel like play - because at TRP, they are.

🎭

Dramatic & Social Play

Pretend play is serious business. Through it, children practice empathy, negotiate with peers, build narratives, and develop the social skills they'll carry for life.

💪

Gross Motor & Movement

Active bodies and active minds go together. Our program builds physical confidence, coordination, and healthy habits through movement woven into every part of the day.

Daily Life

What Does a Day at The Ross Preschool Look Like?

Every day at TRP is a balance of structure and freedom, guided activity and open exploration. Children arrive to a warm, welcoming environment where they're greeted by name, by teachers who are genuinely happy to see them.

Morning might begin with a circle time full of songs, stories, and conversation - followed by project time where children dive deep into an activity of their choice. Outdoor play is never optional; it's woven into the fabric of every day.

Afternoons bring art, music, dramatic play, or science exploration. We close each day with reflection - giving children language and space to process what they experienced, what they created, and what they noticed.

What stays constant is the warmth. The laughter. The deep sense that this is a place where your child is known and genuinely loved.

Beyond the School Year

Summer Camps at The Ross Preschool

When the school year ends, the adventure continues. Our summer camps bring together the same warmth, creativity, and intentional learning that define TRP - in a fun, flexible format perfect for the summer months.

Camps are 100% outdoors with an incredible mix of activities - arts and crafts, STEM projects, read-alouds, dramatic play, water play, and plenty of unstructured time to explore, build friendships, and just be a kid.
